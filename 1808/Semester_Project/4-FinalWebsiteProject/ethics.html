Ethical AI

Countless tools and technologies have been used to aid humans throughout history. Artificial intelligence is one of these tools. AI has numerous applications in the real world. It is used for good when it is supplementing human knowledge, not replacing it.

AI can be used to enhance the intelligence of humans. It was created as a tool to help humans. Its implementation will ultimately determine its characteristics.

Some of the most common forms of ethical AI include suicide prevention chatbots, human resource management, intelligent cybersecurity, and search recommendations. These forms of AI show that ethical AI exists. However, all AI is not ethical.

Unethical AI

Like coding in general, AI operates on a set of hardcoded instructions. It can easily be told to operate in inhumane ways. Artificial intelligence, unlike other forms of human aid, can operate without human intervention.

Thus, unethical AI can solve a beneficial problem, but traverse a destructive route getting there. AI will initially do what it is told. However, once it figures out what to do, it will break from human reliance and learn on its own moving forward.

Feeding machines biased data will teach them to learn our biases, leading to further discrimination. AI learns the lessons we teach it and can also learn the things we did not intend to teach it. Algorithmic bias can cause AI to act in unethical ways as well.

Areas of Failure

AI has lots of positives and will benefit many people. However, it also has lots of negatives. Privacy and security are often not at the center of AI. As AI evolves, its ability to use personal information in ways that can intrude on privacy interests increases almost exponentially. Artificial Intelligence also has numerous security vulnerabilities. Several legal issues surround AI too. The question of governance concerns many people. How will the law respond to the emergence of AI? The legality of AI is not the only thing that is holding it back. Its continued use as a form of data mining is often seen as unethical. Its last negative is its association with social issues. AI often runs into the problem of having to choose what is best for itself as opposed to what is best for others. Forcing a computer to make life-or-death decisions is tragic and should be avoided at all costs.
